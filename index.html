The 5 Best 'One Piece' Story Arcs Ranked

1. Marineford War Arc

Episodes: 457-489

Marineford is the climax of the first half of One Piece, bringing together almost every major character and faction at the time. It’s a heart-wrenching battle, featuring Ace’s death and Whitebeard’s heroic stand. This arc also reveals important lore about the One Piece world, the Yonko, and the Admirals, making it unforgettable.

Rescue Mission

Luffy and many major pirates converge to rescue his brother, Ace, who’s been captured by the Marines and sentenced to death.

Clash of Titans

Whitebeard, one of the Yonko (Emperors of the Sea), leads his crew and allies against the full force of the Marines, including the Admirals and Warlords.

End of an Era

Whitebeard's death shakes the balance of power in the world, leading to a new "Great Pirate Era."

2. Enies Lobby Arc

Episodes: 264-312

The Straw Hats take on the World Government itself to rescue Nico Robin. The emotional weight of Robin’s backstory and her famous “I want to live!” declaration add depth to her character.

Power-Up Moments

Luffy reveals Gear Second and Gear Third, while Zoro, Sanji, and other crew members show their growth through intense battles against CP9, a powerful secret government agency.

"I Want to Live!"

Robin’s plea to be saved is one of the most powerful and emotional moments, strengthening the bond within the crew.

Nico Robin's Backstory

We learn about Robin’s tragic past, her life on the run, and her desire to live despite her fear of putting others in danger.

Declaration of War

The Straw Hat crew wages war against the World Government to save Nico Robin from imprisonment, symbolized by Luffy burning the World Government flag.

3. Wano Country Arc

Episodes: 890-1085

The Samurai of Wano, along with the Straw Hats and their allies, seek to overthrow the tyrannical rule of Kaido, another Yonko.

Through the backstory of Kozuki Oden, we learn about Wano’s rich history and its connection to Roger, the Pirate King, and the ancient secrets of the One Piece world.

Luffy and other characters reach new heights in power, including new techniques and transformations, to take on Kaido’s forces.

Wano is One Piece’s longest arc so far, and it pulls in storylines from the past two decades, like Kaido, the Yonko, and the Samurai of Wano. The arc is an epic blend of Japanese folklore with the series' high-stakes battles and character payoffs, and it showcases Oda’s most intricate world-building yet.

4. Alabasta Arc

Episodes: 92-130

The Alabasta arc is the first large-scale conflict in the series, focusing on Princess Vivi and her mission to save her country from the villainous Crocodile and his Baroque Works. The arc has thrilling fights, an emotional farewell with Vivi, and further emphasizes the theme of friendship and sacrifice.

Crocodile's Plan

Crocodile manipulates Alabasta into civil war to gain control, using his Baroque Works organization to destabilize the kingdom from within.

Nakama Bond

The crew’s bond grows stronger as they risk their lives to save Alabasta, culminating in their heartfelt farewell to Vivi, who decides to stay to rebuild her kingdom.

Important Alliances

The arc highlights key alliances, as we see the Straw Hats fighting alongside Vivi, and even former enemies like Mr. 2 (Bon Clay) become allies.

5. Sabaody Archipelago Arc

Episodes: 385-405

Sabaody reveals the harsh realities of the world, especially regarding the Celestial Dragons, human trafficking, and discrimination. It’s a major turning point where the Straw Hats are scattered by Bartholomew Kuma. It introduces the concept of the Supernovas and sets the stage for the series’ darker second half.

Introduction of the Supernovas

This arc introduces the “Worst Generation,” a group of powerful rookie pirates, including Trafalgar Law, Eustass Kid, and others who will play crucial roles later.

Escalating Stakes

Sabaody acts as a gateway to the series' darker second half, as the world around the Straw Hats becomes more dangerous and complex.
